Training

Cambridge Dogs training classes offers puppy, adult beginner and advanced classes.  Classes are reward-based with no harsh handling and focus on effective communication between dog and handler and an awareness and management of levels of stress in dogs.

We also offer one-to-one training including New Puppy Start, New Rescue Dog Start, rehabilitation and help with specific training problems as well as running workshops, talks and other events.

Initial one-to-ones are £60 for 1 1/4 hours, to allow extra time for assessment, to gather background information and provide a training plan. Follow on individual training sessions are £50 an hour.

Visit our training website Cambridge Dogs for full information about the team and classes.

Any training plan needs to take account of you, your family and lifestyle, your dog's breed, history and individual temperament. The plan will consider your dog's basic needs, including a sense of safety and security, companionship, physical exercise and mental stimulation. Training and advice can include:

  • Management to prevent problems in and outside the home.
  • Basic core obedience training: sit, come, walk nicely on the lead, pay attention, relinquish items willingly and leave items or people/other dogs/animals alone.
  • Establishing a clear communication system based on reinforcement of desired behaviour to reduce anxiety, increase predictability and structure, and enhance co-operation between you and your dog.
  • Teaching your dog to be calm and settle.
  • Finding appropriate ways of enabling your dog to have choice and control over aspects of his or her own life. It's not all about imposing our wishes on dogs!
